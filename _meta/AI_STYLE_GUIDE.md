# AI_STYLE_GUIDE.md

## Writing Goal

Write like an experienced product designer talking to another experienced professional.

Not a marketer.

Not a recruiter.

Not a consultant.

Not an AI.

---

# Desired Tone

Primary:

- Human
- Clear
- Intelligent
- Conversational
- Credible

Secondary:

- Thoughtful
- Strategic
- Practical

---

# Writing Should Feel Like

A strong senior designer explaining how they approached a difficult problem.

---

# Avoid

- Synergy
- Leverage
- Innovative
- Cutting-edge
- Robust
- Best-in-class
- Industry-leading
- Passionate about
- Results-driven
- Thought leader
- Dynamic
- World-class

Avoid corporate language entirely.

---

# Sentence Style

Prefer:

- Short sentences
- Active voice
- Concrete language
- Precise wording

Avoid:

- Dense paragraphs
- Long explanations
- Marketing copy
- Abstract ideas without examples

---

# Portfolio Copy Rules

Do not tell readers Ryan is:

- Strategic
- Collaborative
- Technical
- Thoughtful

Instead demonstrate those qualities through examples.

Show.
Do not tell.

---

# Case Study Writing

Focus on:

1. Context
2. Problem
3. Constraints
4. Decisions
5. Tradeoffs
6. Outcomes
7. Learnings

Avoid:

- Design theater
- Process worship
- Generic UX methodology

Communicate why decisions were made.

---

# Resume and About Content

Prefer evidence.

Bad:

"Strategic product designer passionate about creating innovative experiences."

Good:

"Over the last several years, I've worked on everything from enterprise software to member-facing experiences, helping teams bring structure to complex problems and move ideas into production."

---

# Builder Mindset

When relevant, highlight:

- Prototyping
- Front-end implementation
- Design-to-code workflows
- AI-assisted development
- Collaboration with engineering

Never imply Ryan is a full-time software engineer.

The narrative is:

"A design leader who enjoys building."

---

# Preferred Voice

Imagine:

- Experienced
- Humble
- Direct
- Highly competent

The reader should feel:

"This person knows what they're doing."

Not:

"This person is trying to impress me."

---

# Final Filter

Before generating content, ask:

1. Does this sound human?
2. Does this sound credible?
3. Does this sound like Ryan?
4. Would a hiring manager trust it?
5. Can every claim be defended?

If any answer is no, rewrite it.
